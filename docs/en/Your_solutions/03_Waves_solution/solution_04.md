# Task 04 – Phase Difference

## Problem Statement

What is the phase difference in radians between two points on a wave that are separated by a distance of $\Delta x = \lambda/3$?

## Theory

The phase $\phi$ of a wave is given by the argument of the sine or cosine function, typically $(kx - \omega t)$. For a fixed time, the phase depends only on position $x$:

$$
\phi = kx
$$

The phase difference $\Delta \phi$ between two points separated by $\Delta x$ is:

$$
\Delta \phi = k \Delta x
$$

where $k = 2\pi / \lambda$ is the wave number.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Substitute the Wave Number

$$
\Delta \phi = \left( \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \right) \Delta x
$$

### 2. Calculate using $\Delta x = \lambda/3$

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \cdot \frac{\lambda}{3}
$$

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{3} \text{ radians}
$$

## Final Result

The phase difference is $\frac{2\pi}{3} \text{ rad}$ (or $120^\circ$).

## Interpretation

A full wavelength corresponds to a phase shift of $2\pi$ (a complete cycle). Since the distance is exactly one-third of a wavelength, the phase shift is exactly one-third of a full cycle.
