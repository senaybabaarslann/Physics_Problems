# Task 07 – Standing Wave Modes

## Problem Statement

A standing wave with four antinodes is produced on a string of length $L = 80 \text{ cm}$. What is the wavelength of this wave?

## Theory

For a string fixed at both ends, the number of antinodes corresponds to the harmonic number $n$.
The relationship between $L$ and $\lambda$ is:

$$
L = n \frac{\lambda}{2}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Identify Harmonic Number

Four antinodes means $n = 4$ (fourth harmonic).

### 2. Solve for Wavelength

$$
0.80 = 4 \left( \frac{\lambda}{2} \right)
$$

$$
0.80 = 2\lambda
$$

$$
\lambda = 0.40 \text{ m}
$$

## Final Result

The wavelength is $0.40 \text{ m}$ (or $40 \text{ cm}$).

## Interpretation

In the fourth harmonic, the string is divided into four segments, each being half a wavelength long. Therefore, the total length of the string contains exactly two full wavelengths.
