# Task 06 – Wave Equation Analysis

## Problem Statement

A wave is described by $y(x,t) = 0.05 \sin(2\pi x - 50\pi t)$, where $x, y$ are in meters and $t$ is in seconds. Determine:
a) Amplitude $A$
b) Wavelength $\lambda$
c) Frequency $f$
d) Wave speed $v$

## Theory

The standard form of a traveling wave is:

$$
y(x,t) = A \sin(kx - \omega t)
$$

Comparing the terms:
* $A = 0.05$
* $k = 2\pi$
* $\omega = 50\pi$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### a) Amplitude

By inspection:

$$
A = 0.05 \text{ m}
$$

### b) Wavelength

Using $k = 2\pi / \lambda$:

$$
2\pi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \implies \lambda = 1 \text{ m}
$$

### c) Frequency

Using $\omega = 2\pi f$:

$$
50\pi = 2\pi f \implies f = 25 \text{ Hz}
$$

### d) Wave Speed

The speed is $v = f \lambda$ or $v = \omega / k$:

$$
v = \frac{50\pi}{2\pi} = 25 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Final Result

* **Amplitude:** $0.05 \text{ m}$
* **Wavelength:** $1 \text{ m}$
* **Frequency:** $25 \text{ Hz}$
* **Wave speed:** $25 \text{ m/s}$

## Interpretation

The wave is traveling in the positive x-direction (due to the minus sign between $kx$ and $\omega t$). It has a relatively low frequency and a speed characteristic of waves in strings or slow mechanical waves.
