# Task 01 – Wave Properties

## Problem Statement

A sound wave in air has a frequency of $440 \text{ Hz}$. If the speed of sound in air is $343 \text{ m/s}$, what is its wavelength? What is its wavelength in water, where the speed of sound is $1482 \text{ m/s}$?

## Theory

The relationship between wave speed $v$, frequency $f$, and wavelength $\lambda$ is given by the fundamental wave equation:

$$
v = f \lambda
$$

Frequency is a property of the source and remains constant when a wave passes from one medium to another. Wavelength changes proportionally with the wave speed in the medium:

$$
\lambda = \frac{v}{f}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Wavelength in Air

Given $f = 440 \text{ Hz}$ and $v_{air} = 343 \text{ m/s}$:

$$
\lambda_{air} = \frac{343}{440}
$$

$$
\lambda_{air} \approx 0.7795 \text{ m}
$$

### 2. Wavelength in Water

Given $v_{water} = 1482 \text{ m/s}$:

$$
\lambda_{water} = \frac{1482}{440}
$$

$$
\lambda_{water} \approx 3.3682 \text{ m}
$$

## Final Result

* **Wavelength in air:** $\lambda \approx 0.78 \text{ m}$
* **Wavelength in water:** $\lambda \approx 3.37 \text{ m}$

## Interpretation

As the sound wave enters water, its speed increases significantly due to the higher bulk modulus of water compared to air. Since the frequency is constant, the wavelength must increase to satisfy the wave equation. This explains why sound travels further and faster in denser media.
