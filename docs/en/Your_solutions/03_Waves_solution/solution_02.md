# Task 02 – String Harmonics

## Problem Statement

A guitar string is $64 \text{ cm}$ long and has a fundamental frequency (one antinode) of $330 \text{ Hz}$. What is the speed of the wave on this string?

## Theory

For a string fixed at both ends, the standing wave condition requires that the length $L$ is an integer multiple of half-wavelengths:

$$
L = n \frac{\lambda_n}{2}
$$

For the fundamental frequency ($n=1$):

$$
L = \frac{\lambda_1}{2} \implies \lambda_1 = 2L
$$

The wave speed is related to the frequency and wavelength by $v = f \lambda$.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Convert Units

The length $L$ must be in meters:

$$
L = 64 \text{ cm} = 0.64 \text{ m}
$$

### 2. Determine Wavelength

For the fundamental mode:

$$
\lambda = 2 \cdot 0.64 = 1.28 \text{ m}
$$

### 3. Calculate Wave Speed

Using $f = 330 \text{ Hz}$:

$$
v = f \lambda = 330 \cdot 1.28
$$

$$
v = 422.4 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Final Result

The speed of the wave on the string is $422.4 \text{ m/s}$.

## Interpretation

The wave speed on a string depends on the tension and the linear mass density ($v = \sqrt{T/\mu}$). In this case, the speed is determined by the geometric constraints of the fundamental harmonic.
