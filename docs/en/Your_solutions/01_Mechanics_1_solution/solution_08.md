# Task 08 – Circular Motion

## Problem Statement

Calculate the centripetal acceleration of a person standing on the Earth's equator. The Earth's radius is approximately $R_E = 6378 \text{ km}$.

## Theory

Centripetal acceleration $a_c$ for an object in uniform circular motion is:

$$
a_c = \frac{v^2}{R} = \omega^2 R
$$

where $\omega$ is the angular velocity. For Earth, the period of rotation is $T \approx 24 \text{ hours}$.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Calculate Angular Velocity $\omega$

The period $T$ in seconds:

$$
T = 24 \times 3600 = 86400 \text{ s}
$$

The angular velocity is:

$$
\omega = \frac{2\pi}{T} = \frac{2\pi}{86400} \approx 7.272 \times 10^{-5} \text{ rad/s}
$$

### 2. Calculate Centripetal Acceleration

Radius $R_E = 6.378 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$.

$$
a_c = \omega^2 R_E
$$

$$
a_c = (7.272 \times 10^{-5})^2 \cdot (6.378 \times 10^6)
$$

$$
a_c \approx (5.288 \times 10^{-9}) \cdot (6.378 \times 10^6) \approx 0.0337 \text{ m/s}^2
$$

## Final Result

The centripetal acceleration is approximately $0.0337 \text{ m/s}^2$.

## Interpretation

This acceleration is very small compared to the acceleration due to gravity ($g \approx 9.81 \text{ m/s}^2$). It represents only about $0.34\%$ of $g$. This is why the effect of Earth's rotation on our weight is measurable but small.
