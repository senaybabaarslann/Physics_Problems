# Task 09 – Momentum Comparison

## Problem Statement

Which has greater momentum: a 2-gram fly flying at $10 \text{ m/s}$ or a 60-gram tennis ball moving at $1 \text{ m/s}$?

## Theory

Linear momentum $\vec{p}$ is defined as the product of an object's mass $m$ and its velocity $\vec{v}$:

$$
p = mv
$$

Standard SI units (kg for mass, m/s for velocity) must be used for comparison.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Momentum of the Fly

Mass $m_1 = 2 \text{ g} = 0.002 \text{ kg}$.
Velocity $v_1 = 10 \text{ m/s}$.

$$
p_1 = 0.002 \cdot 10 = 0.02 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}
$$

### 2. Momentum of the Tennis Ball

Mass $m_2 = 60 \text{ g} = 0.06 \text{ kg}$.
Velocity $v_2 = 1 \text{ m/s}$.

$$
p_2 = 0.06 \cdot 1 = 0.06 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}
$$

### 3. Comparison

$$
p_2 > p_1 \implies 0.06 > 0.02
$$

## Final Result

The 60-gram tennis ball has greater momentum.

## Interpretation

Even though the fly is moving ten times faster than the tennis ball, the tennis ball's mass is thirty times greater, leading to a higher total momentum.
