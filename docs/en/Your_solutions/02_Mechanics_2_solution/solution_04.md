# Task 04 – Energy & Momentum

## Problem Statement

A $0.5 \text{ kg}$ block slides down a frictionless track from a height of $3.0 \text{ m}$. At the bottom, it collides and sticks to a $1.5 \text{ kg}$ block, which is initially at rest. What is the speed of the combined mass just after the collision?

## Theory

This problem is solved in two stages:
1. **Energy Conservation:** To find the speed of the first block before impact.
2. **Momentum Conservation:** To find the velocity after the perfectly inelastic collision.

For the slide:
$$
v_1 = \sqrt{2gh}
$$

For the collision:
$$
m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2 = (m_1 + m_2) V_{final}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Speed of Block 1 before Collision

$$
v_1 = \sqrt{2 \cdot 9.81 \cdot 3.0} = \sqrt{58.86} \approx 7.67 \text{ m/s}
$$

### 2. Collision Phase

Given $m_1 = 0.5 \text{ kg}$, $m_2 = 1.5 \text{ kg}$, and $v_2 = 0$:

$$
(0.5) \cdot (7.67) + 0 = (0.5 + 1.5) \cdot V_{final}
$$

$$
3.835 = 2.0 \cdot V_{final}
$$

$$
V_{final} = \frac{3.835}{2} = 1.9175 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Final Result

The speed of the combined mass is approximately $1.92 \text{ m/s}$.

## Interpretation

The collision is perfectly inelastic, meaning kinetic energy is not conserved—much of it is transformed into internal energy (heat/deformation). The momentum, however, is conserved, resulting in a significant reduction in speed as the mass of the moving system increases fourfold.
