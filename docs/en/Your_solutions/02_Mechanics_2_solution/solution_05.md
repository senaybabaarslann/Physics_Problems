# Task 05 – Inelastic Collision

## Problem Statement

A $70 \text{ kg}$ runner moving at $3 \text{ m/s } \hat{j}$ jumps onto a $140 \text{ kg}$ stationary cart. 

* What is the final speed of the cart with the runner? 
* Is kinetic energy conserved in this collision? Explain.

## Theory

In a perfectly inelastic collision, the objects stick together. Momentum is conserved:

$$
\vec{p}_{initial} = \vec{p}_{final}
$$

Kinetic energy $K = \frac{1}{2} mv^2$. Energy is conserved only in elastic collisions.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Final Speed

$$
m_r v_r = (m_r + m_c) V_f
$$

$$
70 \cdot 3 = (70 + 140) \cdot V_f
$$

$$
210 = 210 \cdot V_f \implies V_f = 1 \text{ m/s}
$$

### 2. Kinetic Energy Analysis

Initial Kinetic Energy ($K_i$):

$$
K_i = \frac{1}{2} (70) (3)^2 = \frac{1}{2} (70) (9) = 315 \text{ J}
$$

Final Kinetic Energy ($K_f$):

$$
K_f = \frac{1}{2} (210) (1)^2 = 105 \text{ J}
$$

## Final Result

* **Final Speed:** $1 \text{ m/s}$
* **Energy Conserved:** No. $210 \text{ J}$ of kinetic energy was lost.

## Interpretation

Kinetic energy is not conserved because work is done during the collision to "link" the runner and the cart. This energy is dissipated as heat and sound. In this specific case, exactly $2/3$ of the initial kinetic energy was lost.
