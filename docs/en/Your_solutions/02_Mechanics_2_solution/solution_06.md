# Task 06 – Energy Dissipation

## Problem Statement

A tennis ball is dropped from a height of $2.0 \text{ m}$. After each bounce, it loses $30\%$ of its mechanical energy. To what height does it rise after the second bounce?

## Theory

Mechanical energy at height $h$ is $E = mgh$. If the ball loses $30\%$ of its energy, it retains $70\%$:

$$
E_{after} = 0.70 \cdot E_{before}
$$

Since $E \propto h$, the height after each bounce follows the same ratio:

$$
h_{n} = 0.70 \cdot h_{n-1}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Height after First Bounce

$$
h_1 = 0.70 \cdot 2.0 = 1.4 \text{ m}
$$

### 2. Height after Second Bounce

$$
h_2 = 0.70 \cdot h_1 = 0.70 \cdot 1.4
$$

$$
h_2 = 0.98 \text{ m}
$$

## Final Result

The ball rises to a height of $0.98 \text{ m}$ after the second bounce.

## Interpretation

Energy loss accumulates geometrically. After $n$ bounces, the height is $h_n = h_0 \cdot (0.7)^n$. The rapid decay in height illustrates how non-conservative forces (like internal friction in the ball) quickly deplete the system's gravitational potential energy.
