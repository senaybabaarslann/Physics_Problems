# Task 08 – EM Spectrum

## Problem Statement

List the following types of electromagnetic radiation in order of increasing wavelength: Infrared, Ultraviolet, Microwaves, X-rays, Radio waves, Gamma rays.

## Theory

The electromagnetic (EM) spectrum is classified based on wavelength or frequency. According to the energy equation $E = hf = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$, wavelength is inversely proportional to energy and frequency. Thus, the shortest wavelengths have the highest energy.

## Step-by-Step Solution

To order these by **increasing wavelength**, we must move from the highest energy (shortest $\lambda$) to the lowest energy (longest $\lambda$):

1. **Gamma rays**: Shortest wavelength (highest energy).
2. **X-rays**: Longer than gamma rays.
3. **Ultraviolet**: Longer than X-rays.
4. **Infrared**: Longer than visible light (and UV).
5. **Microwaves**: Longer than infrared.
6. **Radio waves**: Longest wavelength (lowest energy).

## Final Result

The correct order of increasing wavelength is:

$$
\text{Gamma rays} < \text{X-rays} < \text{Ultraviolet} < \text{Infrared} < \text{Microwaves} < \text{Radio waves}
$$

## Interpretation

Wavelengths in this list range from the size of an atomic nucleus (gamma rays) to the size of buildings or even planets (long radio waves). Understanding this order is crucial for identifying how different types of radiation interact with matter.
