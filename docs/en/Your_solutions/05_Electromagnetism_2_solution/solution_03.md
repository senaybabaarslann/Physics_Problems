# Task 03 – Biot-Savart Law for a Wire Segment

## Problem Statement

A small segment of a line wire of length $dL = 0.1 \text{ m}$ carries a current of $3 \text{ A}$. The segment is located at a distance of $0.2 \text{ m}$ from a point $P$. Calculate the magnetic field at point $P$ due to this current segment (assume the segment is perpendicular to the line connecting it to point $P$).

## Theory

The Biot-Savart Law describes the magnetic field $d\mathbf{B}$ generated by a differential current element $I d\mathbf{l}$:

$$
d\mathbf{B} = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi} \frac{I d\mathbf{l} \times \mathbf{\hat{r}}}{r^2}
$$

In magnitude form, where $\theta$ is the angle between the current element and the vector pointing to $P$:

$$
dB = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi} \frac{I dl \sin\theta}{r^2}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

1. **Parameters**:
* $I = 3 \text{ A}$
* $dl = 0.1 \text{ m}$
* $r = 0.2 \text{ m}$
* $\theta = 90^\circ$ (since the segment is perpendicular), so $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$.

2. **Calculation**:

$$
B = \frac{10^{-7} \times 3 \times 0.1 \times 1}{(0.2)^2}
$$

$$
B = \frac{3 \times 10^{-8}}{0.04}
$$

$$
B = 7.5 \times 10^{-7} \text{ T}
$$

## Final Result

The magnetic field at point $P$ is:

$$
B = 0.75 \text{ \mu T}
$$

## Interpretation

This calculation treats the $0.1 \text{ m}$ segment as an infinitesimal element relative to the distance, which is an approximation. In a real scenario, if the segment length is comparable to the distance, integration across the length would be required to account for the changing distance $r$ and angle $\theta$.
