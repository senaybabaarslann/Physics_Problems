# Task 02 – Ampere's Law for Parallel Wires

## Problem Statement

Two long, parallel wires are $10 \text{ cm}$ apart and carry currents of $5 \text{ A}$ in opposite directions. Calculate the magnitude and direction of the magnetic field at a point midway between the wires.

## Theory

The magnetic field $B$ produced by a long, straight wire at a distance $r$ is given by:

$$
B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}
$$

Where:
* $\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7} \text{ T} \cdot \text{m/A}$ is the permeability of free space.
* $I$ is the current.
* $r$ is the radial distance from the wire.

The direction of the magnetic field is determined by the **Right-Hand Rule**: if the thumb points in the direction of the current, the curled fingers indicate the direction of the magnetic field lines.

## Step-by-Step Solution

1. **Geometry and Distances**:
* Total distance $d = 0.1 \text{ m}$.
* Midway point distance from each wire: $r = \frac{d}{2} = 0.05 \text{ m}$.

2. **Determine Directions**:
Assume Wire 1 carries current "up" and Wire 2 carries current "down".
* At the midway point, Wire 1 (left) creates a field $B_1$ pointing into the page.
* At the midway point, Wire 2 (right) creates a field $B_2$ pointing into the page.
Because the currents are in opposite directions, their magnetic fields reinforce each other at the center.

3. **Calculate Magnitudes**:

$$
B_1 = B_2 = \frac{4\pi \times 10^{-7} \times 5}{2\pi \times 0.05}
$$

$$
B_1 = B_2 = \frac{2 \times 10^{-7} \times 5}{0.05} = 2 \times 10^{-5} \text{ T}
$$

4. **Superposition**:

$$
B_{total} = B_1 + B_2 = 2 \times 10^{-5} + 2 \times 10^{-5} = 4 \times 10^{-5} \text{ T}
$$

## Final Result

The magnitude of the magnetic field is:

$$
B = 40 \times 10^{-6} \text{ T} = 40 \text{ \mu T}
$$

The direction is perpendicular to the plane containing the wires.

## Interpretation

In the region between two wires carrying currents in opposite directions, the magnetic fields add up because the circular field lines from both wires point in the same direction in that central space. If the currents were in the same direction, the field at the exact midpoint would be zero.
