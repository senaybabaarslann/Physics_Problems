# Task 07 – Wavelength and Frequency

## Problem Statement

The human eye is most sensitive to light with a wavelength of about $550 \text{ nm}$. What color does this correspond to in the visible spectrum? What is the frequency of this light?

## Theory

The relationship between the speed of light $c$, wavelength $\lambda$, and frequency $f$ is given by the wave equation:

$$
c = \lambda f
$$

Where:
* $c \approx 3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$ (speed of light in vacuum).
* $\lambda$ is the wavelength in meters.
* $f$ is the frequency in Hertz (Hz).

The visible spectrum ranges approximately from $400 \text{ nm}$ (violet) to $700 \text{ nm}$ (red).

## Step-by-Step Solution

1. **Identify the Color**:
A wavelength of $550 \text{ nm}$ is located near the center of the visible spectrum. This wavelength corresponds to **green-yellow** light.

2. **Convert Units**:
The wavelength must be in meters for the calculation:

$$
\lambda = 550 \text{ nm} = 550 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m} = 5.5 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}
$$

3. **Calculate Frequency**:
Rearranging the wave equation for $f$:

$$
f = \frac{c}{\lambda}
$$

$$
f = \frac{3 \times 10^8}{5.5 \times 10^{-7}}
$$

$$
f \approx 5.45 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}
$$

## Final Result

* Color: **Green-Yellow**
* Frequency: $f \approx 5.45 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}$ (or $545 \text{ THz}$)

## Interpretation

The frequency of visible light is extremely high, in the hundreds of terahertz. The fact that the human eye is most sensitive to $550 \text{ nm}$ is an evolutionary adaptation, as this is the peak wavelength of solar radiation reaching the Earth's surface.
