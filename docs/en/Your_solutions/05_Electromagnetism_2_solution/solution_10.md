# Task 10 – Speed of Light in Media

## Problem Statement

What is the speed of light in a diamond, which has an index of refraction $n = 2.42$?

## Theory

The refractive index $n$ of a medium is defined as the ratio of the speed of light in a vacuum $c$ to the speed of light in that medium $v$:

$$
n = \frac{c}{v}
$$

Where:
* $c \approx 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$

## Step-by-Step Solution

1. **Rearrange the Formula**:
To find the speed in the medium $v$:

$$
v = \frac{c}{n}
$$

2. **Substitute Values**:

$$
v = \frac{3.00 \times 10^8}{2.42}
$$

3. **Calculate**:

$$
v \approx 1.2396 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Final Result

The speed of light in diamond is approximately:

$$
v \approx 1.24 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Interpretation

Light travels significantly slower in diamond than in vacuum (less than half the speed). This high refractive index is responsible for the "brilliance" and significant dispersion (fire) seen in cut diamonds, as it causes light to bend sharply and reflect internally more easily.
