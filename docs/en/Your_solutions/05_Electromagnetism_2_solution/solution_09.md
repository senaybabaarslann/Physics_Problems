# Task 09 – Refraction (Snell's Law)

## Problem Statement

A light ray travels from air ($n=1.00$) into glass ($n=1.50$). If the angle of incidence is $30^\circ$, what is the angle of refraction?

## Theory

Refraction is the bending of light as it passes from one medium to another with a different optical density. This is governed by Snell's Law:

$$
n_1 \sin(\theta_1) = n_2 \sin(\theta_2)
$$

Where:
* $n_1, n_2$ are the refractive indices of the first and second media.
* $\theta_1$ is the angle of incidence.
* $\theta_2$ is the angle of refraction.

## Step-by-Step Solution

1. **Identify Constants**:
* $n_1 = 1.00$ (air)
* $n_2 = 1.50$ (glass)
* $\theta_1 = 30^\circ$

2. **Apply Snell's Law**:

$$
1.00 \times \sin(30^\circ) = 1.50 \times \sin(\theta_2)
$$

3. **Solve for $\sin(\theta_2)$**:
Knowing $\sin(30^\circ) = 0.5$:

$$
0.5 = 1.50 \times \sin(\theta_2)
$$

$$
\sin(\theta_2) = \frac{0.5}{1.5} = \frac{1}{3} \approx 0.333
$$

4. **Calculate $\theta_2$**:

$$
\theta_2 = \arcsin(0.333) \approx 19.47^\circ
$$

## Final Result

The angle of refraction is approximately:

$$
\theta_2 \approx 19.5^\circ
$$

## Interpretation

Because the light is moving from a lower refractive index (air) to a higher refractive index (glass), the ray bends **toward the normal**. This results in an angle of refraction that is smaller than the angle of incidence.
