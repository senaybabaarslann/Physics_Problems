# Task 05 – Energy Stored by Charge in a Capacitor

## Problem Statement

We have a parallel-plate capacitor with the following parameters:
* $S = 0.02\,\mathrm{m^2}$
* $d = 5\,\mathrm{mm}$
* $U = 500\,\mathrm{V}$

1. Calculate the capacitance $C$ of the capacitor.
2. Calculate the energy $U_E$ stored in the capacitor.
3. Calculate the electric field intensity $E$ between the plates.
4. Calculate the force of attraction $F$ between the plates.

## Theory

For a parallel-plate capacitor:
* Capacitance: $C = \frac{\varepsilon_0 S}{d}$
* Stored Energy: $U_E = \frac{1}{2} C U^2$
* Electric Field: $E = \frac{U}{d}$
* Attraction Force: $F = \frac{1}{2} \varepsilon_0 E^2 S$

## Step-by-Step Solution

1. **Capacitance Calculation**:

$$
C = \frac{8.854 \times 10^{-12} \times 0.02}{0.005} = 3.5416 \times 10^{-11} \text{ F}
$$

2. **Stored Energy Calculation**:

$$
U_E = \frac{1}{2} \times 3.5416 \times 10^{-11} \times (500)^2
$$

$$
U_E = 0.5 \times 3.5416 \times 10^{-11} \times 250000 = 4.427 \times 10^{-6} \text{ J}
$$

3. **Electric Field Intensity**:

$$
E = \frac{500}{0.005} = 100,000 \text{ V/m}
$$

4. **Force of Attraction**:

$$
F = \frac{1}{2} \times 8.854 \times 10^{-12} \times (10^5)^2 \times 0.02
$$

$$
F = 0.5 \times 8.854 \times 10^{-12} \times 10^{10} \times 0.02 = 8.854 \times 10^{-4} \text{ N}
$$

## Final Result

1. $C = 35.4 \text{ pF}$
2. $U_E = 4.43 \text{ \mu J}$
3. $E = 10^5 \text{ V/m}$
4. $F = 0.885 \text{ mN}$

## Interpretation

The results show that even with a relatively high voltage, the physical force of attraction is quite small. The energy stored is also small, illustrating that capacitors are typically used for high-speed energy release rather than bulk energy storage.
