## 7. Logic & Series (The Fly Problem)
**Problem:** Two bicycles are 10m apart, moving towards each other at 0.5 m/s each. A fly travels back and forth between them at 2 m/s until they collide. What is the total distance the fly travels?

* **Step 1 (Collision Time):** First, we find how long it takes for the bicycles to meet. 
  The relative speed is $0.5 + 0.5 = 1 \text{ m/s}$.
  Time ($t$) = Distance / Relative Speed = $10 / 1 = 10 \text{ seconds}$.

* **Step 2 (Fly's Distance):** The fly travels at a constant speed of 2 m/s during these 10 seconds.
  Distance = Speed $\times$ Time = $2 \text{ m/s} \times 10 \text{ s} = 20 \text{ meters}$.

* **Conclusion:** Instead of summing an infinite series of the fly's paths, we simply multiply the fly's constant speed by the total time before collision. The fly travels **20 meters**.
