## 5. Trigonometry (Vector Decomposition)

**Given:**
* Magnitude ($M$) = 15
* Angle ($\theta$) = 60°

### Task: Find the Horizontal and Vertical Components
To decompose a vector into its components, we use the following trigonometric functions:
* **Horizontal ($x$):** $M \cdot \cos(\theta)$
* **Vertical ($y$):** $M \cdot \sin(\theta)$

---

### Step-by-Step Calculation:

**1. Horizontal Component ($A_x$):**
$$A_x = 15 \cdot \cos(60^\circ)$$
Since $\cos(60^\circ) = 0.5$:
$$A_x = 15 \cdot 0.5 = 7.5$$

**2. Vertical Component ($A_y$):**
$$A_y = 15 \cdot \sin(60^\circ)$$
Since $\sin(60^\circ) \approx 0.866$:
$$A_y = 15 \cdot 0.866 = 12.99$$

---

**Final Result:**
The vector is decomposed into a horizontal component of **7.5** and a vertical component of **12.99**.
