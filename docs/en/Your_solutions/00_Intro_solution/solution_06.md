## 6. Function Analysis
**Function:** $f(x) = 3x^2 - 12x + 7$
**Goal:** Find the critical points and determine if they are local maxima or minima.

* **Step 1 (First Derivative):** To find the critical points, we take the first derivative of the function:
  $f'(x) = 6x - 12$
* **Step 2 (Critical Points):** Set the derivative to zero and solve for $x$:
  $6x - 12 = 0 \Rightarrow 6x = 12 \Rightarrow x = 2$
* **Step 3 (Second Derivative Test):** To determine the nature of the point, we find the second derivative:
  $f''(x) = 6$
* **Conclusion:** Since the second derivative ($f''(x) = 6$) is positive, $x = 2$ is a **local minimum**.
