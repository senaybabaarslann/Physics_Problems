## 9. Optimization Problem (Maximum Area)
**Problem:** A rectangle is under the curve $y = 3 - x^2$ in the first quadrant. Find the dimensions that maximize the area.

* **Step 1 (Define Area Function):** Let a point on the curve be $(x, y)$. The area $A$ of the rectangle is width ($x$) times height ($y$):
  $A(x) = x \cdot y = x(3 - x^2) = 3x - x^3$
* **Step 2 (Find the Derivative):** To find the maximum, we take the derivative of the area function:
  $A'(x) = 3 - 3x^2$
* **Step 3 (Solve for Critical Point):** Set the derivative to zero:
  $3 - 3x^2 = 0 \Rightarrow 3x^2 = 3 \Rightarrow x^2 = 1 \Rightarrow x = 1$
* **Step 4 (Calculate Dimensions):** When $x = 1$, find $y$:
  $y = 3 - (1)^2 = 2$
* **Final Answer:** The dimensions for the maximum area are **width = 1** and **height = 2**. The maximum area is 2.
