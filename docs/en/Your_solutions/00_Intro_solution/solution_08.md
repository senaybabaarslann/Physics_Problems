## 8. Definite Integrals
**Problem:** Calculate the area under the curve of $f(x) = \sin(x)$ from $x = 0$ to $x = \pi$.

* **Step 1 (Antiderivative):** To find the area, we first find the indefinite integral of $\sin(x)$:
  $\int \sin(x) dx = -\cos(x)$
* **Step 2 (Apply Limits):** Now, we apply the upper limit ($\pi$) and the lower limit ($0$):
  $[-\cos(x)]_0^\pi = -\cos(\pi) - (-\cos(0))$
* **Step 3 (Calculate):** Since $\cos(\pi) = -1$ and $\cos(0) = 1$:
  $-(-1) - (-1) = 1 + 1 = 2$
* **Conclusion:** The total area under the sine curve from 0 to $\pi$ is exactly **2**.
