# Task 05 – Field Levitation

## Problem Statement

What electric field strength is required to make a proton levitate against Earth's gravity at the level of the Earth's surface?

## Theory

For a particle to levitate, the upward electric force $\vec{F}_e$ must exactly cancel the downward gravitational force $\vec{F}_g$:

$$
\sum F = F_e - F_g = 0 \implies qE = mg
$$

Where:
* $m_p \approx 1.67 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}$
* $g \approx 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$
* $e \approx 1.60 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Force Balance Equation
The required electric field $E$ is:

$$
E = \frac{m_p g}{e}
$$

### 2. Numerical Substitution
$$
E = \frac{(1.67 \times 10^{-27})(9.8)}{1.60 \times 10^{-19}}
$$

$$
E \approx 1.02 \times 10^{-7}\text{ V/m}
$$

## Final Result

The required electric field strength is:

$$
E \approx 1.02 \times 10^{-7}\text{ N/C (or V/m)}
$$

The field must point upward because the proton has a positive charge.

## Interpretation
This is an extremely weak electric field. For comparison, the fair-weather atmospheric electric field near Earth's surface is roughly $100\text{ to }150\text{ V/m}$, which is billions of times stronger than what is needed to levitate a single proton.
