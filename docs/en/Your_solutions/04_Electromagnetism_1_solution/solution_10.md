# Task 10 – Lorentz Force acting on Wire

## Problem Statement

A straight wire $2.0\text{ m}$ long carries a current of $10\text{ A}$. It is placed in a uniform magnetic field of $B = 0.5\text{ T}$. Calculate the force if the angle $\theta$ is:
a) $90^\circ$, b) $45^\circ$, c) $0^\circ$.

## Theory

The force on a current-carrying wire in a magnetic field is:

$$
F = I L B \sin(\theta)
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Base Calculation
$I L B = (10)(2.0)(0.5) = 10\text{ N}$

### 2. Apply Angles
* **a) $\theta = 90^\circ$**:
$$
F = 10 \cdot \sin(90^\circ) = 10\text{ N}
$$

* **b) $\theta = 45^\circ$**:
$$
F = 10 \cdot \sin(45^\circ) = 10 \cdot \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \approx 7.07\text{ N}
$$

* **c) $\theta = 0^\circ$**:
$$
F = 10 \cdot \sin(0^\circ) = 0\text{ N}
$$

## Final Result
a) $10\text{ N}$
b) $7.07\text{ N}$
c) $0\text{ N}$

## Interpretation
Maximum force occurs when the current is perpendicular to the field. No force is exerted when the current flows parallel to the field lines.
