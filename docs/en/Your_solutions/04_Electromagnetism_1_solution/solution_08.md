# Task 08 – Lorentz Force Magnitude

## Problem Statement

A charged particle with charge $q = 2 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$ and mass $m = 4 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}$ enters a magnetic field of $B = 0.5\text{ T}$ at a speed of $v = 10^6\text{ m/s}$ perpendicular to the field. What is the magnitude of the Lorentz force?

## Theory

The magnitude of the magnetic component of the Lorentz force is:

$$
F = |q| v B \sin(\theta)
$$

Since the velocity is perpendicular to the field, $\theta = 90^\circ$ and $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Identify Variables
* $q = 2 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$
* $v = 10^6\text{ m/s}$
* $B = 0.5\text{ T}$

### 2. Calculation
$$
F = (2 \times 10^{-19})(10^6)(0.5)
$$

$$
F = 1.0 \times 10^{-13}\text{ N}
$$

## Final Result

The magnitude of the Lorentz force is $1.0 \times 10^{-13}\text{ N}$.

## Interpretation
While the force value seems small, the resulting acceleration ($a = F/m$) is approximately $2.5 \times 10^{13}\text{ m/s}^2$, which is massive due to the particle's tiny mass.
