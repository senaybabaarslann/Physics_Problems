# Task 13 – Transformer Ratio

## Problem Statement

Step down $120\,\text{V}$ to $9\,\text{V}$. $N_p = 400$. Find $N_s$.

## Theory

$$
\frac{V_s}{V_p} = \frac{N_s}{N_p}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

1. **Rearrange for $N_s$:**

$$
N_s = N_p \cdot \frac{V_s}{V_p}
$$

2. **Calculate:**

$$
N_s = 400 \cdot \frac{9}{120} = 400 \cdot 0.075 = 30
$$

## Final Result

The secondary coil must have $30$ turns.

## Interpretation

The turn ratio matches the voltage ratio in an ideal transformer.
