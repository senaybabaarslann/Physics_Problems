# Task 07 – Capacitors in Parallel

## Problem Statement

Two capacitors, $C_1=4\,\mu\text{F}$ and $C_2=6\,\mu\text{F}$, are connected in parallel to a $10\,\text{V}$ battery. Calculate the total charge and total energy stored.

## Theory

Equivalent capacitance for parallel connection:

$$
C_{eq} = C_1 + C_2
$$

Total charge:

$$
Q = C_{eq} V
$$

Total energy:

$$
U = \frac{1}{2} C_{eq} V^2
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

1. **Calculate $C_{eq}$:**

$$
C_{eq} = 4\,\mu\text{F} + 6\,\mu\text{F} = 10\,\mu\text{F} = 10 \times 10^{-6}\,\text{F}
$$

2. **Calculate Charge $Q$:**

$$
Q = (10 \times 10^{-6}\,\text{F}) \cdot 10\,\text{V} = 100 \times 10^{-6}\,\text{C} = 100\,\mu\text{C}
$$

3. **Calculate Energy $U$:**

$$
U = \frac{1}{2} (10 \times 10^{-6}\,\text{F}) \cdot (10\,\text{V})^2 = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 10^{-5} \cdot 100 = 5 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{J} = 0.5\,\text{mJ}
$$

## Final Result

- Total Charge: $100\,\mu\text{C}$
- Total Energy: $0.5\,\text{mJ}$

## Interpretation

Capacitors in parallel increase the total energy storage capacity of the system by effectively increasing the plate area.
