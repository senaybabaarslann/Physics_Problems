# Task 08 – AC Voltage Equation

## Problem Statement

$I(t) = 2 \sin(120\pi t)$. Calculate $V(t)$ for a $50\,\Omega$ resistor.

## Theory

According to Ohm's Law in AC circuits, the instantaneous voltage is:

$$
V(t) = I(t) \cdot R
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

1. **Apply Ohm's Law:**

$$
V(t) = [2 \sin(120\pi t)] \cdot 50
$$

2. **Simplify:**

$$
V(t) = 100 \sin(120\pi t)
$$

## Final Result

$$
V(t) = 100 \sin(120\pi t)
$$

## Interpretation

For a purely resistive load, the voltage and current are in phase, meaning they reach their maximum and minimum values at the same time.
